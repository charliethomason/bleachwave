Summary: When I first moved to Chicago in Summer 2008, I was hired as a full-time Intern and Production Assistant with Ow MyEye Productions. For the previous 4 years, Joe Winston and Laura Cohen had been working on an independent documentary feature film based on Thomas Frank’s NYTimes-bestselling book “What’s the Matter with Kansas?” At that time, the film was still “in development” and I was responsible for building the site’s initial web presence. This included a complete website, social media strategy, Wikipedia page, IMDb (Internet Movie Database) listing, and YouTube video series. The website was one of the first “complete” websites that I had designed and developed by myself. I used HTML, CSS3, and some JavaScript (from an open-source JS library) so that the site could be unique and interactive, as well as accessible by mobile devices. The project introduced me to CSS3 rollovers, Lightbox image viewers, iframes, Internet Explorer 6.0 compatibility, and several Internet marketing concepts and standards. The documentary went on to be called “one of the 10 best documentaries of 2009” by Roger Ebert and is currently available on Netflix, Hulu, Amazon On-Demand and DVD. The website has since been redesigned, although a re-creation of my original version can be found by clicking the “View Site” button at right.
