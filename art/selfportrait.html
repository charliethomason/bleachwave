Self-Portrait, 2007

  • Medium: Graphite on beige-colored paper
  • Size: 16” x 20”
  • Year: 2007
Summary: Although I’ve done many self-portraits in my life, this is the only one I’ve ever done with a mirror instead of a photograph. More importantly perhaps, it looks the most like me of any of my self-portraits. Near the end of my last semester of art school, I decided it had been too long since I’d completed a self-portrait. For this piece, I taped a sheet of tan paper to a drawing board and mounted it up on an easel. Next to that, I place a large mirror up on a shelf. They were positioned so that I only had to move my eyes from the mirror back to the paper and my head could stay perfectly still (easier said than done!). This drawing was completed with a broad variety of pencils and under the supervision of Sal Villagran. I gave the final framed piece to my mother as a gift.
